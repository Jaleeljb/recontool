2. CORE INTELLIGENCE MODULES

01. DNS INTELLIGENCE

Reveals mail server infrastructure (MX), authoritative nameservers, and SPF/DMARC records. [cite_start]Identifies email providers (e.g., Google Workspace) and vendor relationships [cite: 41-46].

02. TECH STACK DETECTION

Detects CMS (WordPress, Drupal), JS frameworks, server technologies, and analytics tools. [cite_start]Helps assess security maturity and potential vulnerabilities [cite: 52-58].

03. EMPLOYEE INTELLIGENCE

Uncovers corporate email patterns (e.g., first.last@) and LinkedIn profiles. Essential [cite_start]for understanding organizational structure and social engineering vectors [cite: 63-68].

04. RELATED DOMAINS

Finds subdomains, development environments, and sister companies via Certificate [cite_start]Transparency logs and Reverse WHOIS [cite: 75-77].

05. HISTORICAL DATA

Tracks domain age, ownership history, and DNS changes over time. Helps identify stability [cite_start]or recent suspicious transfers [cite: 87-94].

06. GEOLOCATION

Pinpoints physical server location, ISP, and data center providers. Detects if "local" [cite_start]companies are actually hosted offshore [cite: 101-108].

07. WHOIS DATA

Extracts registrant details, expiration dates, and admin contacts. Connects digital [cite_start]identities to real-world personas [cite: 117-125].

08. HTTP HEADERS

Analyzes server versions and security headers (CSP, HSTS). Reveals misconfigurations and [cite_start]outdated software [cite: 131-138].

09. SSL/TLS CERTIFICATES

Examines issuers and Subject Alternative Names (SANs) to find hidden related domains on [cite_start]the same certificate [cite: 146-154].

10. METADATA & SEO

Scrapes meta tags, Open Graph data, and analytics IDs to map business structures and [cite_start]potential acquisitions [cite: 159-166].

11. CONTACT INTEL

Aggregates emails, phones, and social handles (GitHub, Twitter) to map the digital [cite_start]footprint of the organization [cite: 174-181].
